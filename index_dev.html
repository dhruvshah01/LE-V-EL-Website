About

In machine graphical perception, computers decode low-level building blocks of visualizations. Recently, researchers started studying how different artificial neural networks perform the human perception experiments of Cleveland and McGill. To allow consistent evaluations and meaningful comparisons, we present the LE-V-EL benchmark repository.For all benchmarks, we offer training datasets, including labels and challenge datasets, without labels. Researchers can submit solutions for the challenge datasets and receive a leaderboard ranking through a scalable submission mechanism that computes scores based on the loss function. As the initial population of the leaderboard, we include rankings for existing neural network architectures such as AlexNet, VGG-19, DenseNet, and a combined Mask-RCNN/VGG-19 instance segmentation pipeline. LE-V-EL is fully open and allows fair comparisons of future machine graphical perception algorithms.

Download Dataset

The dataset contains 8 elemenatary stimuli that are inspired from the Cleeveland Mcgill experiments. Each image is 100x150 with 4 stimuli of different quantitative value. This repository provides standardized datasets that cover the graphical perceptions tasks of Cleveland and McGill. These include decoding elementary perceptual tasks from 1984 and ratio estimations from 1985.

Amazon Mechanical Turk

We also provide the human benchmark of all the stimuli from amazon turker. The results are obtained from various user with masters certification. These annotaion results can help the users understand the visual cortext perception.
